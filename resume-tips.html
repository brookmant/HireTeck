Resume Tips

Do not feel like you need to be an English Professor or A+ student to compose a solid resume. You need to represents your skill set and provide details about your career not write a New York Times bestselling novel!

Resume Tips and Advice

  1. Resumes should be an organized list your duties, accomplishments and major job functions. Sit down, gather details/take notes and narrow them down to a concise list that describes you but doesn’t repeat itself over and over. Don’t write a book but don’t leave major key points out in fear of going the resume being too long. IT resumes are different than other industries. Consider a project addedum if you fear going overboard with information.

  2. Get all dates for each employer and education all correct. If you fudge on dates, it will come back to bite you during the application process. You don’t want to lose that one great job due to simple mistakes. After you organize all your thoughts, start putting the ideas to paper. You might create 5 or 10 resumes before the final draft is complete. Don’t stress out..It’s normal!

  3. All Technical or IT related resumes should have a list of “tools” or “technologies” used on the job. Our clients and customers like to see a skills summary along with those skills mixed into the wording of your resume. It clarifies your experience and allows the audience to understand where, when and how much you’ve used certain “tools”.

  4. Resumes are general business documents not reports or books. Employers are very concerned with spelling errors and simple grammatical mistakes but fragments are allowed. The proper formatting, consistency and sharp wording are best. Use easy to understand English words, big words make you look like your hiding something or you’re just a bragger!

  5. Use Action verbs and avoid pronouns. Action verbs help you sell yourself in a quick, sharp manner. The audience knows you who are talking about; avoid pronouns like “I” or “me”.

  6. Always update your resume and don’t forget to update your online resources and HireTech’s database if you change jobs or make enhancements.

  7. Make sure to keep all resumes consistent across any social media platforms. Don’t post a different resume on LinkedIn.com than you might have used on Monster or CareerBuilder and never send a potential employer 2 different resumes for the same job. Always eliminate red flags or you will be eliminated.

If all else fails, seek professional help! HireTech is able to offer great resume writing services and help with the entire process for a small fee.
